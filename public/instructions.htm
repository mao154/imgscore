Instructions

Task: Scoring Image

On the next pages, you will be asked to score a series of images.

Image scoring is done in sets. Each set contains _ images, and you can do up to _ sets:

  • you need to score at least 1 set, so that you can gain experience in making relative comparisons;
  • you cannot score more than _ sets, because we want to collect assessments from multiple people.

Important! You will only be compensated for complete sets.

Scientific Research

The images you are asked to score have been created by human subjects participating in a scientific experiment.

To ensure the integrity of this scientific research, please try to be as accurate as possible in your assessments.

Scoring Scale

Score each image by moving a slider on a scale from 0 (lowest grade) to 10 (highest grade) on four dimensions:
  • Creativity
  • Interestingness as a face
  • Abstractness
  • Overall appeal or quality

How the images have been created

Each image has been created in less than 60 seconds using a special computer interface. This interface is designed to draw images quickly, but with some limitations.

You will see a random sample of the created images in the next page.

Random Sample of Images

Remember: the images were created by human subjects during a scientific study.

Please take some time to review the images carefully.

In this task, you are asked to assess not the absolute quality, but relative to other images created in the same task. We show you this sample to give you a sense of the images you will see.
